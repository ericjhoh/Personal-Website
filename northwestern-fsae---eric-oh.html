Overview

As the Ergonomics Sub-Team Lead for Northwestern University's Formula SAE team, I am responsible for designing critical components of our vehicle to ensure safety and intuitive usability.

Our sub-team optimizes:
          1. The floors
          2. Seat
         3. Firewall
          4. Dashboard

These directly impact driver comfort, safety, and performance. To bring these designs to life, I have utilized SolidWorks to create precise CAD models for the vehicle floors. Our floor manufacturing plan employs advanced materials to balance strength, weight, and durability: a half-inch Nomex honeycomb core sandwiched between three layers of carbon fiber on both the top and bottom surfaces. In addition, I delegate tasks and hold preliminary design reviews with my team to incorporate the different ideas we have. This project offers a hands-on opportunity to apply mechanical engineering principles while collaborating with a team of more than 60 undergraduate and graduate students. Through this experience, I’ve honed my technical skills, contributed to an ambitious team mission, and gained insight into the intersection of engineering, safety, and human-centered design.

Eric Oh @ 2024
