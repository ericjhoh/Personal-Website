Overview

I contributed to the development and testing of a 2D-planar cable-driven parallel robot prototype designed for applications in physical therapy. This experience introduced me to the foundational aspects of robotics and engineering, sparking my interest in precision mechanisms and their real-world applications. Working within a multidisciplinary team, we utilized tools such as Python for simulation, Autodesk Inventor for CAD modeling, and Arduino to control and program the system. The project also incorporated a PID controller to enhance the accuracy of the motors, which precisely adjusted the string lengths that dictated the robot’s movements. Through iterative testing and prototyping, we refined the robot’s precision to better suit the demands of physical therapy. I gained hands-on experience with technical tasks that included wiring components to the PID controller, adjusting parameters for optimal motor control, and assisting in the assembly and testing phases. While my responsibilities were entry-level, this internship provided me with valuable exposure to robotics, programming, and collaborative problem-solving in a professional research environment. This experience not only introduced me to the practical applications of mechanical engineering concepts but also solidified my interest in pursuing innovative solutions.
